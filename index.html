WORK

BYOBuddy

beer & food pairing app

http://byobuddyand.me

Role

UX Design • Back End Development • Front End Development

Details

From October to December 2011, I was a part of Code Academy, a 12-week intensive course that teaches “passionate people how to build [Ruby on Rails] web applications." BYOBuddy is a food and beer pairing application designed for Code Academy’s Demo Day, where it was presented to an audience of 260 at Chicago’s TechNexus.

Process

To build BYOBuddy, I began by producing Balsamiq wireframes to map out user experience. Then, I built the backend with Ruby on Rails, created application mockups in Photoshop, and styled the app with HTML, CSS, and JQuery. Finally, I deployed BYOBuddy via Heroku.

Tech Skills

Ruby on Rails • HTML • CSS • JQuery

Tools

Balsamiq • Photoshop • Git • TextMate • Heroku

Vanberg & DeWulf Site Re-launch

belgian beer importers

http://www.belgianexperts.com/

Role

Content Migration Strategy • Content Management • Project Management • Social Media Strategy • E-mail Marketing

Details

Vanberg & DeWulf is the oldest Belgian beer importer in the United States and has been importing specialty beers from Belgium and Northern France since 1982. In 2011, Vanberg & DeWulf hired Blueprint Design Studio, a Chicago-based web design agency, to migrate and redesign their old site into a sleek, new WordPress-powered site

Process

In order to meet the strict deadlines of the website re-launch I created and maintained a detailed website content inventory to keep communication open between Blueprint and Vanberg & DeWulf and to ensure that content was migrated successfully. Then, I reviewed and edited content in an iterative editing process, negotiating input and revisions from Vanberg & DeWulf stakeholders.

After the site went live, I led a web and social media campaign (Facebook, Twitter, e-mail marketing campaigns) to alert our audiences – beer and food writers, bloggers, bars, stores, restaurants, distributors, sales reps, beer enthusiasts, etc. – of the re-launch of the Vanberg & DeWulf’s site and entrance into the social media realm.

Post-launch, I was responsible for the continual content management and upkeep, as well as the development of communications strategies – via site, social media channels, or e-mail marketing campaigns – for our different constituencies.

Tech Skills

HTML • CSS • WordPress

Tools

Photoshop • InDesign • MailChimp • HootSuite

Vanberg & DeWulf Coast to Coast Toast

the biggest belgian beer bash of the millennia

Sample e-mail marketing campaigns and print media

Role

Promotional Marketing • Content Strategy • Competitive Analysis • Graphic Design

Details

To celebrate 30 years of importing Belgian Beer, Vanberg & DeWulf threw the “biggest Belgian beer celebration of the millennia” on November 15, 2011. That evening, beer enthusiasts raised their glasses in 350 establishments across the nation to salute the importer and America’s craft beer culture.

Process

We started competitive analysis for our November Coast to Coast Toast (C2CT) 6 months before the event. After conducting research based on sales data from our distributors, industry publications, and competitor advertising, I coordinated teams of interns for our call outreach efforts. Although our initial goal was to have 200 accounts participate in the C2CT, our outreach approach nearly doubled our initial goal and we toasted with 350 accounts instead.

To keep our “Coast to Coast Toast Hosts” up to date on the latest news and bring more “hosts” to the fold, I managed weekly e-mail marketing campaigns via MailChimp. Additionally, I created a number of materials to help our hosts with their toasts – table tents, posters, party-planning toolkit, and beyond.

Tech Skills

HTML • CSS • WordPress

Tools

Photoshop • InDesign • MailChimp • HootSuite
